4 Axis Robot

3D Design, Motor Control, C++ and Python

What?
A robotic arm designed completely from scratch. Programmed the robot to place blocks in squares 1-9 of a tic-tac-toe board as a test of its functionality. I also programmed tic-tac-toe logic so that the robot could play against a user.
How?
Used Fusion 360 to model all the components, which were then 3D printed. An Arduino board was used to control the micro servo motors. Programmed motor control with C++ and the game logic with python.
Results?
The robot could process the user's chosen position, decide its next move, pick up a block, and accurately place it. The robot's logic ensured it was unbeatable in gameplay.

Automated Culinary Robotics System

Mechatronics Design

What?
A robot designed to create a customized cup of pudding for a quarter by combining pudding powder, water, and sprinkles. Depending on user specifications, the robot could make different types of pudding.
How?
Programmed user input, sensor logic, and motor control using C++. Built the robot using LEGO Technic components and 3D-printed parts modeled in Fusion 360.
Results?
Built a fully functional robot capable of detecting a quarter, processing user input for flavor preferences, accurately moving cups via conveyor, dispensing ingredients, and mixing the pudding. Earned recognition from the professor as the most intricate project in the class.

Filtration System
for Remote Communities

Project Management

What?
Led a team of 5 to design a water filtration system for underserved northern communities, aiming to produce over 100L/day of clean water.
How?
Programmed an Arduino-controlled chlorination and agitation system in C++ for one-touch disinfection; modeled and 3D printed compact electronic housing in Fusion 360 for seamless pump integration.
Results?
Delivered clear, drinkable water from heavily soiled sources, surpassing the 100L/day goal. Project was recognized as the top performer in the cohort for filtration efficiency and system reliability.

12ft Trebuchet!

Creative and Resourceful Design

What?
Designed and built a 12-ft, 200 lb trebuchet capable of handling 150 lb dynamic loads and launching projectiles over 250 ft. Led a cross-generational design team—including my grandma—to blend medieval mechanics with modern determination.
How?
Applied statics and energy transfer calculations to optimize arm length and counterweight ratio; sourced and repurposed recycled materials for all structural components; relied entirely on hand tools and practical problem-solving—no computers required.
Results?
Produced a fully operational trebuchet that consistently hurled projectiles beyond 250 ft, built entirely from recycled scrap. The project showcased resourceful engineering and sustainable construction.

Drone Capture Device

Mechanical Engineering

What?
Designed and built a lightweight net gun system intended for portable drone defense, keeping the total system weight under 0.5kg for field deployability.
How?
Modeled 8 custom components in Fusion 360 optimized for FDM 3D printing; iterated the trigger mechanism through 20+ CAD revisions to improve actuation smoothness and ensure mechanical reliability.
Results?
Achieved consistent net deployment in field tests with improved accuracy and reliability compared to initial prototypes, demonstrating feasibility for compact counter-drone applications.

Octopus Inspired Gripper

Modeled in Fusion

What?
I am currently working on a pulley-actuated robotic gripper. Taking inspiration from the tentacles of an octopus, this gripper should easily grip a large variety of unique shapes.
How?
Designed using Fusion 360, I've begun 3D printing prototypes with the goal of perfecting the joint tolerances. The arms will be actuated using pulleys and a stepper motor. The grip strength will be monitored by tension load cells attached to the pulley.
Results?
Coming soon! The final prototype should grip a variety of objects with a specified force, so as not to damage delicate objects.
